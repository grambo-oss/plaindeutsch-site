This is your annual Nebenkostenabrechnung (utility cost statement) for the 2024 calendar year. 🔍What to check
  • Heating allocation key (Verteilerschlüssel) — By law, at least 50% of heating costs must be split by actual consumption (your heat meters), not purely by floor area. Check the allocation key shown in the Heizkosten breakdown. If it says 100% by area, this may be unlawful.
  • 12-month deadline — Your landlord must send the Nebenkostenabrechnung within 12 months of the billing period ending (i.e., by 31 December 2025 for the 2024 period). If it arrived late, you generally do not have to pay any Nachzahlung — though you still get any refund owed.
  • Garden costs in your lease — Gartenpflege (€212.00) is only chargeable if it is explicitly listed in your rental agreement (Mietvertrag). Check your lease's Betriebskosten clause.
  • No unusual one-off items — The charges listed are all standard recurring Betriebskosten. Repair and renovation costs (Instandhaltung) are not present, which is correct — these cannot be passed on to tenants.
📌What to do next
  1. 1
    Check the deadline first. Confirm when you received this statement. If it was delivered after 31 December 2025, you are not legally required to pay the Nachzahlung. If it arrived on time, you have 30 days to pay.
  2. 2
    Request receipts (Belege einsehen). You have a legal right to inspect the original receipts behind every charge. Write to your landlord requesting a Belegeinsicht. This is especially worth doing for the Hausmeister/Hausreinigung line (€398.50), which is the item most commonly inflated.
  3. 3
    If something looks wrong, contact a Mieterverein. Germany's tenants' associations (Mieterverein) offer professional, low-cost review of Nebenkostenabrechnungen. Annual membership (~€80–120) covers a full review. Find your local branch at mieterschutzbund.de or mieterbund.de.
